Concejo Abierto
A system that organizes the information of the City Council for the Secretary of Government.



Github Demo Poster Thesis

Objective

Develop a tool for the Town Hall to keep political control over Bogota's Council. Automatize processes that are currently done by hand. Make it public both as a product and as an API.

Problem Statement

Bogotá's government is divided into the Town Hall, led by the Mayor, and the Council, divided into three commissions. These two entities have huge tensions between them because they must keep control over the other and function independently. Currently, the Town Hall employs a big team to inform on any occurrence in the Council, but this team has been lagging due to their poor system. At least one team member must be in every session of the Council (which often happen on weekends and holidays) and take note of everything in an Excel form created by the team, and then, through the livestream of the session, confirm that all was properly written down. This information is then processed by a single engineer to be stored in a single drive, creating a bottleneck and an obvious point of failure.


After several interviews with the team and stakeholders, deep data diving into the current datastore and design thinking over the problem at hand, Concejo Abierto was born. Concejo Abierto aims to make this process much less error prone by automatically processing the input and creating a system that not only the Town Hall uses, but empowers citizens and researchers.


Deliverables

1. A working and live product that organizes the information given. This is currently at Concejo Abierto
2. A full technical reference for the first product. This was delivered to the main client and is not publicly available.
3. A recommendation sheet with a clear process definition to help the team in charge in their work.
